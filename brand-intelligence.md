## Identity and visual grammar

Lab37's public presence is stark, industrial, technical, and editorial: black and white, restrained navigation, condensed-feeling display typography, stainless equipment photography, product/software compositions, and technical blueprint linework. A standalone official logo file suitable for local nominative use was not found, so the campaign uses the clearly typeset company name `LAB37` above the fold with an immediate independent-candidate qualifier.

## Official imagery

The visible product image and blueprint are loaded from official Lab37/Framer asset URLs through `remote-fallback.css`. Local placeholder targets prevent broken markup. The publication workflow is retained so these official files can later be pinned byte-for-byte under `assets/brand/` when GitHub Actions is available.

## Color and typography

Source-sampled recognition layer: near-black `#050505`, white `#ffffff`, paper neutral `#f4f4f1`, and stainless neutrals. Candidate-original accents are subordinate and role-derived: lime for decision readiness, amber for food behavior, coral for operator intervention, and blue for software/order truth.

No proprietary font files are copied. The implementation uses broadly available condensed sans fallbacks for display, Helvetica-family body text, and monospaced technical labels while reproducing the official site's uppercase, tracked, industrial character.

## Original candidate visual system

The Recipe-to-Release Assembly is candidate-created. Five contract gates—food behavior, mechanics, order truth, operator flow, and economics—remain independently visible while the system responds to scenario and pressure controls. It is not a Lab37 logo, proprietary internal interface, or claim of internal product health.

## Independent-candidate distinction

Every candidate-facing surface identifies the work as a candidate vision, application material, interview brief, or entry plan by Russell Dudek. The site footer states that it is not an official Lab37 publication or endorsement.
