## Visible company identity

The public Lab37 site uses a highly restrained, typeset `LAB37` identity in black-and-white navigation and editorial layouts. A crisp standalone official logo file suitable for local nominative use was not discoverable through the official public pages during this build. The candidate site therefore uses a clearly typeset `LAB37` company name above the fold, paired immediately with `Candidate vision by Russell Dudek` language. It does not trace, redraw, or claim an invented logo.

## Typography evidence and decision

The official site presents a geometric / neo-grotesque industrial sans character with uppercase navigation, generous tracking, condensed-feeling display headlines, strong black-and-white contrast, and editorial scale changes. No licensed public font package was identified for redistribution.

Implementation:

- Display: broadly available condensed sans fallbacks (`Arial Narrow`, `Helvetica Neue Condensed`, `Roboto Condensed`, `Franklin Gothic Medium`).
- Body: `Helvetica Neue`, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif.
- Technical labels: `IBM Plex Mono`, `SFMono-Regular`, Consolas, monospace fallbacks.

No proprietary font files are copied or shipped. Fidelity is expressed through uppercase behavior, tracking, weight, condensed display scale, and dense technical labels.

## Photography, illustration, and interface character

- Large, crisp product photography with equipment and software visible together.
- Technical blueprint linework and dimensional annotation.
- Black / white editorial pages with industrial restraint.
- Sparse interface chrome and generous negative space.
- Product truth shown through physical equipment, order software, and operational context rather than decorative technology imagery.

## Geometry and motion translation

The candidate-created `Recipe-to-Release Assembly` is derived from the role: five physical contract gates sit above a bowl and ingredient particles traverse the complete path only as the gates clear their acceptance conditions. It is not a company logo, proprietary product rendering, or imitation of an internal UI.

The motion language is deliberate, mechanical, and explanatory:

- contract gates widen, shift, and clear in response to product pressure;
- ingredient particles move through the complete system;
- the visitor changes a scenario or pressure and sees the decision posture and evidence categories update;
- reduced-motion mode renders a complete static state.
