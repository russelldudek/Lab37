Read the system. Prove the contract. Make learning repeatable.

Operating posture
Begin with tactical empathy and direct observation. Respect the product knowledge already held by engineering, culinary, manufacturing, field teams, customers, and operators. Add value by making cross-functional decisions and evidence easier to see.

90-day outcomes

Shared system map

One legible view of product dependencies, decision rights, trusted signals, and critical evidence gaps.

Complete decision loop

One pilot or release carried from requirement through acceptance, launch readiness, field evidence, and roadmap action.

Product review cadence

A useful rhythm connecting roadmap, release, pilots, telemetry, exceptions, customer insight, and economics.

Reusable launch memory

A pilot-to-release record and onboarding blueprint that make the next site or recipe easier than the last.

Guiding principles

Observe before prescribingSpend time where Bowl Builder is designed, assembled, tested, loaded, operated, cleaned, serviced, and experienced.
One decision at a timeUse artifacts to clarify the next meaningful product decision, not create documentation volume.
Exceptions are product dataReview modifications, interventions, edge ingredients, faults, and recovery because averages hide the scaling work.
Units and context matterEvery KPI or target needs a definition, baseline, operating context, source, and decision it supports.
Learning must returnEvery pilot and release should close with a requirement, roadmap choice, operating standard, or clearly bounded next experiment.

Stakeholder listening map

Mechanical / electrical

Architecture, component constraints, reliability, access, fault behavior, validation, and design trade-offs.

Software / data

Order flow, recipes, device state, telemetry, bowl records, cameras, alerts, release management, and observability.

Culinary / food science

Ingredient preparation, environmental ranges, dispenser parameters, quality, hold time, modifications, and menu development.

Manufacturing / quality

Build sequence, supplier constraints, inspection, test, configuration, documentation, DFM, and feedback into design.

Support / field / customer

Install, onboarding, training, intervention, cleaning, issue context, recovery, support burden, and restaurant experience.

Commercial / leadership

Customer value, pricing assumptions, utilization, labor and waste economics, roadmap commitments, and investor narrative.

30- and 60-day review questions

At day 30

  • What did I misunderstand before direct observation?
  • Which decisions lack a shared evidence record?
  • Which signals are trusted enough to act on?
  • Where is product learning already strong?

At day 60

  • Did the artifact make a real decision easier?
  • Did every function see its conditions and ownership?
  • Did the pilot or release produce actionable evidence?
  • What should standardize, change, or remain contextual?

Day-90 leadership readout

What I learned

How the real system behaves, where the strongest product mechanisms already exist, and which public assumptions changed after direct observation.

What moved

The decisions, requirements, pilot evidence, readiness conditions, or operating behaviors materially improved.

What scales

The product-contract elements, evidence views, review cadence, onboarding steps, and learning records worth standardizing.

What comes next

A prioritized roadmap and next-quarter plan grounded in customer value, technical dependencies, product evidence, economics, and team capacity.

Success statement

After 90 days, the goal is not a larger collection of product documents. It is a clearer operating system for product decisions: the team can see what the complete product must do, which evidence matters, who owns the trade-off, what a pilot learned, and how that learning changes the next release or deployment.
