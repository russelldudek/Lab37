Bowl Builder is not simply a robot. It is a controlled food-production system for turning order intent into food that is accurate, safe to operate, traceable, recoverable, and economically repeatable.

Lab37 appears to be at an inflection point. LinkedIn reports a fast-growing, engineering-heavy team with a 0.5-year median tenure, while the role asks one PM to connect a multi-year roadmap, market research, competitive analysis, detailed requirements, end-to-end release management, customer-facing pilots, KPIs, business outcomes, and executive narrative. That makes the opportunity less about backlog administration and more about installing product clarity without slowing invention.

At Vape-Jet, I lead product-facing operating work in a software-enabled robotics and machine-vision environment with approximately 1,000 fielded robots. I connect fleet signals, customer support, non-reported error codes, production, quality, purchasing, software issue flow, Jira, ERP/Odoo, training, and leadership review into clearer triage, acceptance, release-readiness, and learning mechanisms. The practical discipline is consistent: make the signal visible, identify the decision, clarify ownership, and return evidence to the product.

I also bring direct food and grocery launch experience from Amazon. I served on the Prime Now launch team with Whole Foods, earned a Food Safety Management certification while at Amazon, and led Prime Pittsburgh execution in a 24/7 customer-backward environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. That work taught me that a food-related customer promise depends on more than speed: quality, safety awareness, staffing, flow, escalation, standard work, and recovery must operate as one system.

Earlier, at Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, validation, documentation, and customer delivery in high-reliability electronics. Together, these experiences give me a unique transferability profile for Lab37: fielded robotics, food fulfillment and safety literacy, electromechanical manufacturing, and product decision systems.

For our conversation, I created an independent candidate vision at russelldudek.github.io/Lab37/. It demonstrates how I would approach the role through an Order-to-Outcome Trace, the Bowl Builder Product Contract, a pilot evidence model, and a 90-day entry plan. These are hypotheses for discovery—not claims about undisclosed Lab37 processes—and are intended to make my reasoning concrete.

My thesis is simple: every release should make Bowl Builder easier to trust, easier to operate, and easier to scale. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how I could contribute across customer discovery, roadmap choices, PRDs and acceptance criteria, pilot ownership as the primary customer interface, firmware/cloud/UX trade-offs, business cases and pricing, KPI visibility, and product narratives for executives, investors, customers, partners, and live demos.
