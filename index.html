01 · The mandate beneath the title

Make a promising robotic makeline repeatable across recipes, sites, operators, and economics.

Lab37 publicly describes a modular line where orders arrive through software, ingredients dispense by weight, cameras add intelligence, each bowl leaves a digital record, and the system closes the package.

The product question is not merely “Did the robot make a bowl?” It is: Did the complete system produce the intended food, at the intended pace, with recoverable behavior, a clear evidence trail, and economics that earn the next deployment?

Every release should make Bowl Builder easier to trust, easier to operate, and easier to scale.

05 · Pilots as product instruments

A pilot should answer the next roadmap decision.

  1. Define the decisionName the roadmap, release, recipe, support, or commercial decision.
  2. Establish the baselineRecord context, units, assumptions, and acceptance thresholds.
  3. Instrument the whole systemConnect bowl output to machine state, software, operator, service, and economics.
  4. Review exceptions firstInspect modifications, edge ingredients, interventions, recovery, and support burden.
  5. Convert evidence to ownershipClose with a requirement, roadmap action, operating standard, or next experiment.
