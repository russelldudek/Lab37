01 · The mandate beneath the title

Make Bowl Builder deployments repeatable across recipes, sites, operators, food-safety controls, and economics.

Lab37 publicly describes a modular line where orders arrive through software, ingredients dispense by weight, cameras add intelligence, each bowl leaves a digital record, and the system closes the package.

The product question is not merely “Did the robot make a bowl?” It is: Did the complete system produce the intended food, within its operating controls, at the intended pace, with recoverable behavior, a clear evidence trail, and economics that earn the next deployment?

Every release should make the food promise safer to operate, easier to trust, and easier to scale.

02 · The system boundary may be larger than the machine

06 · Pilots as product instruments

A pilot should answer the next roadmap decision.

  1. 01Define the decisionName the roadmap, release, recipe, support, or commercial decision.
  2. 02Establish the baselineRecord context, units, assumptions, and acceptance thresholds.
  3. 03Instrument the whole systemConnect bowl output to order state, food controls, machine state, operator action, service, and economics.
  4. 04Review exceptions firstInspect modifications, edge ingredients, interventions, recovery, quality, and support burden.
  5. 05Convert evidence to ownershipClose with a requirement, roadmap action, operating standard, or next experiment.
